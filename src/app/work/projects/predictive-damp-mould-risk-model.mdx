## Overview

This is my MSc Data Science & AI dissertation in partnership with **Believe Housing Company**, one of the largest social housing providers in the North East of England. The goal: build an end-to-end machine learning pipeline that proactively identifies properties at risk of damp and mould — directly supporting compliance with **UK Awaab's Law (2023)**.

## The Problem

Following the tragic death of Awaab Ishak in 2020, UK social landlords are now legally required to identify and remediate damp and mould before tenant health is affected. The challenge: housing officers need to prioritise inspections across thousands of properties — but reactive maintenance is too slow, and human judgement alone misses high-risk cases.

## My Approach

I am building a complete ML pipeline that combines:

- **IoT sensor data** (temperature, humidity, CO₂)
- **Asset records** (property age, build type, EPC ratings)
- **Customer data** (tenancy patterns, repair history)

Across **thousands of social housing properties** in County Durham, I am comparing **Logistic Regression**, **Random Forest**, and **Gradient Boosting** classifiers — engineering features around environmental, structural, and tenancy signals.

## The Output

The model produces **risk tier outputs (Low / Medium / High)** designed for non-technical housing officers — enabling them to prioritise inspections and interventions without needing to interpret raw model probabilities.
